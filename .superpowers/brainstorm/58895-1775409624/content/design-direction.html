Which design direction should we build on?

Each approach carries different tone and credibility signals. Consider who's landing here: someone Googling "ReCODE Medical" out of curiosity.

A

Dark Navy / Gold (Current Live Site)

Tone: Premium, serious, confident

Strengths: The color palette is distinctive and polished. Gold accents on dark navy reads as high-end. The noise texture and radial glow add subtle depth.

Weaknesses flagged: Text-only with no visual hierarchy beyond font weight. HR dividers doing all the structural work. Founder section is a flat text list. No logo/brand mark presence.

Upgrade path: Keep the palette, add real typographic hierarchy, styled founder cards, a proper logo treatment, and the email capture. This is probably the smallest delta from current to polished.

B

Light Editorial (Mockup 02)

Tone: Clean, transparent, editorial

Strengths: Cream/paper feel reads as honest and open. Good for a "here's what happened" narrative. Feels less like a tech startup and more like a professional announcement.

Weaknesses: Can look generic without strong typography. Paper texture might feel dated. Less memorable than the dark option.

Upgrade path: Sharpen the typography, add structured sections, lean into the editorial quality with better spacing and founder treatment.

C

Split Contrast (Mockup 03)

Tone: Bold, modern, dramatic

Strengths: Dark hero + light body creates natural visual hierarchy. The contrast draws the eye down the page. Feels more designed than the other two.

Weaknesses: Two-tone split can feel like a template if not executed well. Needs strong content in both halves to justify the structure.

Upgrade path: Use the dark section for the announcement headline + email capture, light section for the letter and team. Creates a natural narrative flow.

My recommendation: A (Dark Navy / Gold), upgraded

The palette already works. It's distinctive, it reads as premium, and it's live. The problems are structural (no visual hierarchy, flat founder section, missing elements) not chromatic. Upgrading the existing direction is less risky and less work than switching palettes, and the dark/gold combination is more memorable than either alternative for a "what happened to this company" landing page.
